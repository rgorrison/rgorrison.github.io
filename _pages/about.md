Hello and welcome to my personal website. I am a PhD candidate at the University at Albany, in the [Department of Atmospheric and Environmental Sciences](https://www.albany.edu/daes) working under the advisement of Dr. Mathias Vuille. My research is focused on large-scale circulation using an isotopic lens, drawing on terrestrial archives, observations, and isotope-enabled climate models to assess climate variability and change. My current research is centered on South America. I'm interested in how past atmospheric dynamics have changed and how both external forcing and internal variability influence climatic changes.

Since much of my work involves confronting changes in our climate, it is important to me to move beyond documenting and observing the climate crisis. To that end, I also engage in climate advocacy campaigns and a variety of outreach activities. 

If you want to discuss research, advocacy, or are a student with questions about your own path forward, please reach out!

I also enjoy art galleries, boxing, cooking, dancing, and eating baked goods.
